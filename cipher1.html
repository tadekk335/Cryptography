In cryptography, the Polybius square is a device invented by the Ancient Greek historian and scholar Polybius, His tool allowed letters to be easily signaled using a numerical system. This idea also lends itself to cryptographic manipulation and steganography. The original square used the Greek alphabet, but it can be used with any other alphabet. By cross-referencing the two numbers along the grid of the square, a letter could be deduced, e.g. "NAT" becomes "33 11 44". Because 26 characters do not fit in a square, it is rounded down by combining two letters (usually C and K or I and J). By itself the Polybius square is not very secure however a Polybius square offers the possibility of fractionation and becomes a useful component in some other ciphers such as the Nihilist cipher or the Bifid cipher.
